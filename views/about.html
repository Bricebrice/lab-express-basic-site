About Sam

Sam Altman, born on April 22, 1985, is an American entrepreneur and investor. He has been the CEO of OpenAI since 2019, with a brief period of dismissal in November 2023. Prior to OpenAI, Altman served as the president of Y Combinator from 2014 until 2019. Altman co-founded Loopt at the age of 19, which was later acquired by the Green Dot Corporation. He became a partner at Y Combinator in 2011 and was named president in 2014.

In 2019, Altman co-founded Tools For Humanity, working on a global iris-based biometric system called Worldcoin. He has been involved in various initiatives, including funding Project Covalence during the COVID-19 pandemic. Altman invests in technology startups and nuclear energy companies, and he briefly served as the CEO of Reddit in 2014.

Altman is known for his involvement in political projects, such as "The United Slate" in 2018 and supporting Andrew Yang's presidential campaign in 2019. He made a world tour in May 2023, meeting leaders and diplomats in 22 countries. Altman has received several awards and recognitions, including being named one of Time magazine's 100 most influential people in 2023.

In his personal life, Altman is a vegetarian, gay, and in a relationship with Oliver Mulherin. He resides in San Francisco and owns a weekend home in Napa, California. Altman is also a prepper, with a keen interest in preparedness items.
